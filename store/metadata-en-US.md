## Description

```
Talk to your passenger — clearly, hands-free, and without buying €200 of
intercom hardware.

TwoUp connects two iPhones directly to each other over Wi-Fi Aware, Apple's
peer-to-peer Wi-Fi technology. No internet, no SIM, no servers, no account:
your voice goes phone-to-phone, encrypted, and never touches a network. It
works in tunnels, in the mountains, abroad without roaming — anywhere two
iPhones are.

HOW IT WORKS
• Use the Bluetooth helmet headsets you already own (or any headset)
• Full-duplex conversation with echo cancellation and noise suppression —
  talk naturally, no push-to-talk
• Keeps working with both phones locked in your pockets
• Pair once; connects automatically every ride and heals itself after
  dropouts
• Glove-sized mute button, honest connection status
• Battery-friendly: a two-hour ride barely registers

PRIVATE BY DESIGN
Your voice never leaves the two phones — there are no servers for it to
leave to. No account, no analytics, nothing collected. The app is open
source, so you don't have to take our word for it.

PRICING
Free to try: every intercom session runs for 15 minutes, restart as often as
you like. One inexpensive lifetime unlock removes the limit — with Family
Sharing enabled, one purchase covers both of you. No subscription, ever.

REQUIREMENTS
Two iPhones on iOS 26 or later — both must support Wi-Fi Aware (TwoUp
checks at first launch and tells you if one doesn't) — one per helmet, each
paired with its own Bluetooth headset. Built for rider and passenger on one
motorcycle — also handy on bicycles, boats, ski slopes, and anywhere else
two people within Wi-Fi range want an open voice line without cell
coverage. Note: music playback pauses while an intercom session runs and
resumes when you end it.
```
